Latitude vs. Max. Temperature


It would be expected that the highest temperatures would be concentrated around the equator at 0 latitude and that temperatures would be evenly distributed around the equator, but this is not the case. Temperatures in latitudes south of the equator are signifigantly higher than in latitudes north of the equator, because at the time of analysis it was summer in the southern hemisphere and winter in the northern hemisphere.
